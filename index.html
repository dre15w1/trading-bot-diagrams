Trading Bot — Architecture Diagrams

Five views of the system, from the full lifecycle down to individual subsystems. Grounded in the live code.

1 · Overall process

The trading loop: find a candidate, generate a signal, gate it, size and route it, manage the position, close it, and feed the result back into the nightly retrain.

2 · Strategies

Andre's multi-timeframe strategy is the live signal engine. Others exist as backtested/optional engines. Every signal is then checked by the AI confirm layer.

3 · How the AI confidence score is calculated

A per-symbol Gradient Boosting model turns ~46 engineered features into a bull/bear probability, which becomes the confidence label, size, and exit style.

4 · Order routing to the brokers

Market data has a broker toggle; execution is always Schwab. Every signal opens a paper position, and (if routed live) real orders on each linked Schwab account, sized independently.

5 · Nightly retrain

At ~17:00 ET the manager swaps the watchlist first, then the trainer builds a fresh model per symbol across the full universe plus swap-contention candidates.

6 · Scale-in / scale-out & take-profit

On each monitor tick the position is checked, in order, against three gates: add on a winning pullback, bank half at target, then exit by mode.

7 · Picking new watchlist candidates

Each night the S&P-500 pool is scored on two axes, gated for tradability, and admitted via a fast-track (breakout) or a 3-night interest streak.
